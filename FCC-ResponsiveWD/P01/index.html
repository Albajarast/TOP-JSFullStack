Severo Ochoa

Co-discoverer of the mechanisms in the biological synthesis of ribonucleic acid (RNA) and deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA).

Here is a time line of Severo Ochoa's life:

  • 1905 - Born in Luarca (Asturias), Spain.
  • 1912 - His dad pass away and his family moves to Málaga, Spain.
  • 1923 - Start studying at University of Madrid Medical School.
  • 1927 - Spend the summer at Glasgow working with D. Noel Paton on creating metabolism improving his english skills.
  • 1929 - Completes his undergraduate medical degree and move to Berlin-Dahlem to join Otto Meyerhof's laboratory.
  • 1930 - Returns to Madrid to complete his research thesis.
  • 1931 - Defends his thesis, marry Carmen García Cobián and move to London to start his postdoctoral study at London National Institute for Medical Research.
  • 1933 - Return to Madrid to study glycolysis in hearth muscle.
  • 1936 - Return to Meyerhof's Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Biology now relocated in Heidelberg.
  • 1940 - Work at the Faculty of Medicine of Washington University in St. Louis.
  • 1942 - Appointed as Research Associate in Medicine at the New York University School of Medicine.
  • 1945 - Become Assistant Professor of Biochemistry.
  • 1946 - Become Professor of Pharmacology.
  • 1947 - Become Professor of Biochemistry.
  • 1954 - Become Assistant Professor of Biochemistry and Chairman of the Department of Biochemistry.
  • 1956 - Become American citizen.
  • 1959 - Awarded with the Nobel Prize for Physiology or Medicine "for their discovery of the mechanisms in the biological synthesis of ribonucleic acid and deoxyribonucleic acid".
  • 1956 - Become Assistant Professor of Biochemistry and Chairman of the Department of Biochemistry.
  • 1978 - Receive the U.S. National Medal of Science.
  • 1985 - Return to Spain after many years of continued research om protein synthesis and replication of RNA viruses. Also, gave advice to the Spanish science policy authorities.
  • 1986 - His wife pass away.
  • 1993 - Die on Madrid on 1st November.
